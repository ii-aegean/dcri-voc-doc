Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

DCRI-voc is a vocabulary of terms related to conservation and restoration interventions. It includes specialised terminology about intervnetion materials, techniques and equipment, as well as their characteristics. The main objective of DCRI-voc is the representation of specialised terminology which can be integrated to a formal ontology about decision-making of conservation and restoration interventions. To this aim it constitutes one of the modules of this ontology along with DCRI-ont and DCRI-ont-spe. A secondary objective of the vocabulary is the development of a vocabulary for the domain of conservation and restoration interventions exploiting Semantic Web technologies, which can be reused in other applications of conservation data management and organisation.

Introduction back to ToC

The DCRI voc, which directly imports SKOS [https://www.w3.org/2009/08/skos-reference/skos.html] and includes individuals which express types of different basic concepts of the decision-making process of the conservation and restoration interventions of cultural heritage (CnR-DM-I) domain (e.g., types of materials, types of CnR interventions, types of damages). While it has been developed in order to be used as part of the ontology, it can also be used independent of it, as a SKOS vocabulary for the CnR-DM-I domain. It includes a number of individuals which capture specialized knowledge regarding two case studies: i) the cleaning of superficial deposits and ii) the consolidation of painting gouache (A-box knowledge). Those individuals are related to specific types of different basic concepts of the ontology. The individuals related to the specific types of concepts were individuals of the SKOS class Concept. In cases where a term is narrower or broader compared to other terms, then the respective individuals are interrelated through the SKOS object properties has_narrower/has_broader.
